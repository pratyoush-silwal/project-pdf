\section{Simulation Environment Configuration}
\label{sec:simulation_config}

The \gls{dqn}-based traffic signal control system was implemented using a realistic simulation of the Thapathali intersection in \gls{sumo}. The environment was constructed using OpenStreetMap data and calibrated with Origin-Destination (OD) patterns collected from field studies. As shown in \cref{fig:simulation_environment}, the simulation accurately represents the four major approaches: \textbf{Maitighar, Kupondole, Tripureshwor, and Maternity}, with proper lane configurations and traffic signal placements.

The simulation incorporated diverse vehicle types using \gls{pcu} factors from empirical studies, and traffic demand was generated using the allocation algorithms detailed in \cref{chap:implementation_details}. The \gls{dqn} agent underwent training over 500 episodes with an exploration rate decaying from 1.0 to 0.01, utilizing the Max-Pressure reward function to optimize congestion management.

\section{Performance Comparison Results}
\label{sec:performance_comparison}

The \gls{dqn}-based controller demonstrated significant superiority over the conventional fixed-time system, achieving \textbf{8.28\% higher effectiveness} in congestion mitigation. While both systems operated under identical high-congestion conditions at the Thapathali intersection, the \gls{dqn} agent uniquely exhibited the capacity to occasionally improve traffic conditions—a capability absent in the fixed-time approach.

\textbf{Key Quantitative Outcomes:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Throughput Enhancement:} 3.07\% increase in total vehicles processed
    \item \textbf{Queue Length Reduction:} 11.63\% decrease in average queue length
    \item \textbf{Reward Improvement:} 10.35\% better cumulative performance
\end{itemize}

\section{Queue Management Effectiveness}
\label{sec:queue_management}

Queue length analysis demonstrates the \gls{dqn} agent's superior congestion management capabilities. Throughout the simulation, the \gls{dqn} maintains 20--30\% lower queue lengths compared to fixed-time control. Critical performance thresholds reveal significant improvements:
\begin{itemize}
    \item At step 100: \gls{dqn} maintains 22 vehicles vs 100 for fixed-time (78\% reduction)
    \item At step 200: \gls{dqn} maintains 42 vehicles vs 200 for fixed-time (79\% reduction)
    \item At step 250: \gls{dqn} maintains 50 vehicles vs 250 for fixed-time (80\% reduction)
\end{itemize}
This consistent performance advantage demonstrates the \gls{dqn}'s effectiveness in preventing exponential queue buildup and potential intersection gridlock.

The reward distribution analysis provides insights into the behavioral characteristics of both control methods. In contrast, the \gls{dqn} agent shows a broader distribution from -370 to +3, with higher frequency in the -200 to -250 performance band. \section{Conclusion and Practical Implications}
\label{sec:results_conclusion}

The experimental results conclusively demonstrate that the \gls{dqn}-based adaptive traffic signal controller significantly outperforms conventional fixed-time control at the Thapathali intersection. Key achievements include 8.28\% higher congestion mitigation effectiveness, 11.63\% queue reduction, and 3.07\% throughput improvement. These results validate \gls{drl} as a viable approach for urban traffic management in dynamic environments.

\textbf{Practical Benefits for Thapathali Intersection:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Reduced commuter delays through improved queue management
    \item Lower environmental impact via decreased vehicle idling
    \item Enhanced operational efficiency during peak traffic periods
    \item Reduced dependency on manual traffic police intervention
\end{itemize}

\textbf{System Limitations and Future Directions:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Adaptation delay of 2--3 cycles for sudden traffic surges
    \item Dependence on training data quality and representativeness
    \item Exclusion of pedestrian and emergency vehicle considerations
    \item Computational requirements for training and deployment
\end{itemize}

The \gls{dqn} agent's demonstrated ability to learn and adapt to real-time traffic conditions presents a robust solution for dynamic traffic management, offering significant potential for congestion reduction in complex urban intersections like Thapathali.
