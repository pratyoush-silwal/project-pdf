\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{ABSTRACT}

Traffic congestion at the Thapathali intersection is a significant issue, causing delays, inefficiencies, higher environmental impacts, and increased travel stress for commuters. Manual traffic control, primarily handled by traffic personnel, relies on the fixed-time signal method and personal judgment, often without considering real-time congestion levels, leading to suboptimal performance under varying traffic conditions. This project proposes an intelligent traffic control system that uses \gls{drl} to optimize traffic flow at the intersection. A \gls{drl} agent based on the \gls{dqn} algorithm is developed and trained in a \gls{sumo}-simulated environment to learn optimal traffic signal timing policies. The system receives input data such as the \gls{od} vehicle count, while other traffic metrics like average waiting times, throughput, and average travel time are calculated within the \gls{rl} framework to inform decision-making for controlling the traffic signal. The system's performance is compared with the fixed-time control method, showing improvements in reducing traffic delays, improving throughput, and optimizing overall intersection performance. The proposed system is designed to reduce queue lengths and throughput enhancing traffic flow efficiency.
